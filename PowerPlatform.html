Power Platform Products

I particularly enjoy working with Microsofts Power Platform.

The below are just a selection of Power Apps I have created whilst working at Springboard IT.

HOLIDAY REQUEST APP

An app for requesting, editing, viewing and accepting/rejecting holiday requests.

The app is in use at multiple companies, the largest having 70+ employees. Holiday balances are by the hour and the app caters for different shift patterns and rota days.

The app features login functionality (via a pin/password set by each employee). This helps differentiate between multiple users using one email address (for example workshop@) and helps catalogues users as either a Team Member or Team manager.

Members can request holiday and edit or view their holiday requests. In addition, Managers can accept/reject holiday requests and view holiday for their specific team (or if part of higher managements/HR) the whole company.

All holiday data is held in SharePoint, with accepted holiday requests appearing on a Calendar view which is also visible in the Company's Microsoft Teams (where the app is also embedded).

Power Automate

There are multiple flows in Power Automate:

  • On submit of a holiday request (and the request being patched to a SharePoint list) a flow ascertains who an individual's manager is and emails them details of the request, with accept/reject options.
    If accepted:
    • An email is sent to the requestee confirming their holiday has been approved, the request is updated to accepted in SharePoint, and it appears on the Calendar view.

AGGREGATE REPORTING APP

This Power App has been designed as a reporting application, bringing together data from seven separate SharePoint lists into a single, easy-to-use view. By consolidating this information in one place, users can quickly understand the overall picture without needing to navigate between multiple systems or lists.

The customer already uses a suite of Power Apps as part of their day-to-day processes. For that reason, the reporting solution was deliberately built as a Power App, rather than a standalone Power BI report. This ensures a consistent user experience, keeps everything within the same Microsoft 365 ecosystem, and allows the report to sit naturally alongside existing applications and workflows.

Power Automate

The report can be exported to PDF on demand using a simple button within the app. When the export button is clicked, it triggers a Power Automate flow that generates a PDF version of the report and automatically sends it to the intended recipient via email. This makes it easy to share up-to-date, formatted reports with stakeholders who may not have access to the app itself.
